Problem definition

"Product managers at tech companies have abandoned any consistent habit of tracking AI research and news — not because they lack interest, but because no existing solution filters and frames information for their specific role."

Problem statement · derived from discovery · April 2026
Who

Primary: Product managers at tech companies working on or adjacent to AI products. They understand why AI matters. They've tried existing solutions and found them either too shallow, too broad, or too dense. So they've stopped trying altogether.

Beachhead segment

PMs actively job-hunting or recently placed in AI PM roles — highest motivation, highest willingness to adopt, most likely to evangelize. Natural wedge before expanding to employed PMs.

The cost

They miss product opportunities and pivots they should be proposing. They waste the limited reading time they have on content that doesn't connect to their daily decisions. The anxiety is chronic, not situational.

No consistent habit

The user isn't optimising an existing routine — they've given up entirely. The product isn't competing with newsletters. It's filling a vacuum.

Willing but unsupported

When asked "would you read it immediately?" — the answer is yes. The user is not the problem. The available solutions are. This is a product gap, not a behaviour change problem.

Opportunity framing

The anxiety is about missing opportunities, not lacking knowledge. Value prop is "never miss what matters to you" — not "understand AI better."

Design constraints
≤ 5 min
Total weekly reading time to feel genuinely caught up
Mon AM
Ideal delivery window — before the week's meetings
1 role
Takeaway framed for their job, not generic summaries
Scope boundaries
Not a news aggregator Not a paper summariser Not another newsletter Not built for engineers in v1 Not a daily product Weekly cadence PM lens only in v1 Replaces habit, doesn't add to it Actionable over comprehensive
Hypotheses to test in user research
H1 — Relevance over volume

PMs will engage consistently if content is pre-filtered to their role — even if it covers fewer topics than existing newsletters.

Test: show 5 role-specific takeaways vs 20 generic headlines. Measure preference and recall.
H2 — Monday morning delivery

Monday morning is the highest-value delivery window because PMs are preparing for the week ahead, not winding down.

Test: ask interviewees when they're most receptive to new information and describe their Monday routine.
H3 — Opportunity framing over knowledge framing

The anxiety is about missing opportunities, not lacking knowledge — meaning the core value prop is "never miss a relevant development" not "understand AI better."

Test: ask interviewees to describe the last time they felt behind, and what the actual consequence was.
