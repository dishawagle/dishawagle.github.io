My perspective

How I think about AI products

"How do you spec a feature when the AI output isn't guaranteed?"

You spec the guardrails, not the output. Define what failure looks like, how the product degrades gracefully, and what the human fallback is. Non-determinism is a design constraint, not an excuse.

"What makes an AI feature trustworthy?"

Transparency about what it can and can't do. Users don't need to understand transformers — they need to know when to trust the output and when to double-check. That's a UX problem as much as a model problem.

"How does your engineering background help you as a PM?"

I can read a technical spec, spot unrealistic estimates, and have honest conversations about trade-offs. I don't need things translated. That builds trust with eng teams quickly.
